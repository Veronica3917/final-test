Designer of bronze heads

The bronze heads in the the Old Summer Palace was cast during the Qianlong period of the Qing Dynasty, designed by the court's Western painter, the Italian Lang Shining, supervised by the Frenchman Jiang Youren, and made by the court craftsmen.

Lang's initial intention was to create a nude female sculpture with a western twist, but the Qianlong emperor felt that this was contrary to Chinese ethics and ordered a redesign. Lang Shining then took into account Chinese folk culture and replaced the human sculptures commonly used in Western fountain designs with seated figures of the twelve zodiac signs.
